Pasta alla Panna

Description

Alla panna simply means "with cream sauce" in Italian. This cream sauce can be likened to a French b√©chamel except no flour is used. The sauce thickens by reduction. It can be used over pasta, meats, and vegetables. Consider a chicken and mushroom dish.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces butter
  • 4 minced garlic cloves or chopped shallots (optional)
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh Italian parsley (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound uncooked pasta of your choice

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt butter. Add garlic or shallots, if using, and saute until translucent.
  2. Add cream, whisking constantly, heating until bubblind and thickened.
  3. Add optional parsley, basil, and salt and pepper to taste, whicking until the sauce is velvety smooth.
  4. Keep sauce warm until ready to use.
  5. Cook pasta according to package directions.
  6. Divide pasta intu four servings and pour warm sauce on top.

Note: By adding 1 cup of Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese to step 3, above, you will have an Alfredo sauce.
